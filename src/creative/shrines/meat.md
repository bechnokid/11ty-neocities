Welcome to the shrine dedicated to an iconic shape for a slab of meat! Often depicted as a piece of meat covering a thick bone, this shape is known in modern times as "anime/manga meat", "caveman meat", or "that meat from One Piece".

I'm not sure why I made this shrine other than the fact that I just wanted to talk about how much I love this edible shape. It's adorable, unique, and sparks joy within me in such a way that I can't describe.

## Appearance
This piece of cylindrical red meat is rather unique in that each end of the bone protrudes from one side of the meat. It cannot naturally appear in this form, no matter what kind of animal is butchered or what kind of method is used to prepare it.

Regardless, the bone that sticks out from both ends of the meat makes it easy for the consumer to hold onto the meat while eating.

The color of the meat can change depending on how cooked it is, with red typically being its rawest state, orange/brown being well-done, and dark brown/black being burnt.

While the bone usually stays a consistent shape, the meat itself can appear in a variety of different shapes, such as ovals and cylinders. They can even take on lumpier forms, making them resemble marshmallows or pillows.

## History

The meat's origins are a mystery, with no real way to pinpoint exactly when and where it first appeared, but some of its earliest depictions can be traced back to [*First Human Giatrus*](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/First_Human_Giatrus), originally published in 1965 with a 10-year run.

It has since appeared in a variety of media from all parts of the world from the East to the West, used as a common prop for particularly gluttonous characters, such as Monkey D. Luffy from *One Piece*.

Most recently, it has appeared in many video games as an item to eat or as a cooking ingredient to replenish the player's health or stamina. While those pieces of meat can be eaten raw, just like their real-life counterparts, it's recommended to cook the meat to perfect for maximum benefits.
