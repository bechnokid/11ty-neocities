title: A New Non-Digital Companion
---
I've never been an animal person.

Well...I like reptiles, amphibians, and most marine life. So, I think it would be more accurate to say that I'm not a "furry" animal person.

Even after moving in with my partners and getting used to their two dogs Ava (RIP) and Nix, I'm still not really into furry animals. This isn't to say that I dislike them or anything, but if I were to have my very own pet, I'd rather it be a ball python.

All that changed on March 17th, when both my partners heard a little kitten meow at our front door during a pretty bad cold front. She first appeared at the door when my husband was awake at 11pm on the 16th, but when he opened the door, she dashed away. She appeared and meowed at the door again at around 2am on the 17th when my wife was awake. Although she almost dashed away again when my wife opened the door, she was eventually brave enough to enter our house.

Imagine my surprise waking up at 5am and hearing my wife tell me, "Bec...we have a little visitor" and proceed to show the little kitten in her lap.

I never thought this would ever happen to me, but I immediately fell in love with her!!! 💖

Oh goodness, I can't describe how much of a good kitty she is. Even though she has the usual hyperactive personality of an adolescent cat, she's so patient, kind, and very well-adjusted around humans!

I wanted to keep her so badly, but there was that looming possibility that she might belong to someone else. That fear quickly disappeared after taking her to the vet and finding out she wasn't chipped. I nearly cried in joy after finding out that we could actually keep her!!!

Seeing as how we got her on St. Patrick's Day, part me wonders who ended up being the lucky ones that day: the little kitty...or us?

Regardless, here are some pictures of the newest member in our family: **Katie Boots!**
